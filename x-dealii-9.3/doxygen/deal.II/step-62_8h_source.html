1592  * We loop again over the degrees of freedom of the cells to
1593  * calculate the system matrix. These loops are really quick
1594  * because we have already calculated the stiffness and mass
1595  * matrices, only the value of @f$\omega@f$ changes.

1599  * {
1600  * for (unsigned int j = 0; j < dofs_per_cell; ++j)
1601  * {
1602  * std::complex<double> matrix_sum = 0;
1603  * matrix_sum += -std::pow(omega, 2) *
1604  * quadrature_data.mass_coefficient[i][j];
1605  * matrix_sum += quadrature_data.stiffness_coefficient[i][j];
1606  * cell_matrix(i, j) += matrix_sum * quadrature_data.JxW;
1607  * }
1608  * cell_rhs(i) += quadrature_data.right_hand_side[i];
1609  * }
1610  * }
1611  * cell->get_dof_indices(local_dof_indices);
1612  * constraints.distribute_local_to_global(cell_matrix,
1613  * cell_rhs,
1614  * local_dof_indices,
1615  * system_matrix,
1616  * system_rhs);
1617  * }
1618  *
1619  * system_matrix.compress(VectorOperation::add);
1620  * system_rhs.compress(VectorOperation::add);
1621  * }

2349 
2350 A phononic cavity is characterized by the
2351 [resonance frequency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resonance) and the
2352 [the quality factor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Q_factor).
2353 The quality factor is equal to the ratio between the stored energy in the resonator and the energy
2354 dissipated energy per cycle, which is approximately equivalent to the ratio between the
2355 resonance frequency and the
2356 [full width at half maximum (FWHM)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Full_width_at_half_maximum).
2357 The FWHM is equal to the bandwidth over which the power of vibration is greater than half the
2358 power at the resonant frequency.
2359 @f[
2360 Q = \frac{f_r}{\Delta f} = \frac{\omega_r}{\Delta \omega} =
2361 2 \pi \times \frac{\text{energy stored}}{\text{energy dissipated per cycle}}
2362 @f]
2363 
2364 The square of the amplitude of the mechanical resonance @f$a^2@f$ as a function of the frequency
2365 has a gaussian shape
2366 @f[
2367 a^2 = a_\textrm{max}^2\frac{\omega^2\Gamma^2}{(\omega_r^2-\omega^2)^2+\Gamma^2\omega^2}
2368 @f]
2369 where @f$f_r = \frac{\omega_r}{2\pi}@f$ is the resonance frequency and @f$\Gamma=\frac{\omega_r}{Q}@f$ is the dissipation rate.
2370 We used the previous equation in the jupyter notebook to fit the mechanical resonance.
2371 
2372 Given the values we have chosen for the parameters, one could estimate the resonance frequency
2373 analytically. Indeed, this is then confirmed by what we get in this program:
2374 the phononic superlattice cavity exhibits a mechanical resonance at 20GHz and a quality factor of 5046.
2375 The following images show the transmission amplitude and phase as a function of frequency in the
2376 vicinity of the resonance frequency:
